Desire conjugation


In japanese, we've learned that there are 3 kind of verb (ichidan, godan, and irregular)
now those verb can have 1 more sub category, they can either be transitive or intransitive.

Transitive verbs are the verb that has someone/something that is doing the action (active agent).
を particle is used to indicates transitive verb

Noun を Verb

In the other hand.

Intransitive verbs happens on their own without something/somebody's action (no active agent).
(your imaginary girlfriend is not included.)
が particle is used to indicates transitive verb

Noun が Verb

here are some of the verb Example

Transitive :

日本語 英語
消けす to turn off, to erase, to delete
止とめる to stop
止やめる to stop

Intransitive :

日本語 英語
消きえる to be off, to be erased, to be deleted
止とまる to be stopped
止やむ to be stopped

Example :

Transitive :

ゆりこ は 電気でんき を 消けしました。
Yuriko turned off the light.

In this case, Yuriko became the active agent.
Even if you remove 「ゆりこ は 」as long as the verb is intransitive, it will
automatically refer to the speaker themselves or the person who are in the topic converstion.

電気でんき を 消けしました。
I turned off the the light.

Intransitive :

電気でんき が 消きえました。
The light went off.

Transitive :

立花たちばな は 学校がっこう の 前まえ で バス を 止とめます
Tachibana will stop the bus infront of the school.

Intransitive :

バス が 止とまりました。
the bus has stopped.

バス が 学校がっこう の 前まえ で 止とまりました。
the bus has stopped The infront of the school.

雨あめ が 止やみました。
The rain has stopped.

2. 止とめる・止やめる ＆ 止とまる・止やむ

If you noticed all of this kanji basically has the same meaning, but has a slightly different usage.
we have 2 verb for "to stop" in transitive part, it has the same kanji but different reading but.

止とめる is used to stop something temporarily, for example stopping a bus, we know that the stop will continue

meanwhile,

止やめる is used to discontinue something, in other word, it will not continue once stopped.

now the intransitive part,

止とまる is used for a machine such as car and train.

止やむ is used for nature such as rain and snow.
