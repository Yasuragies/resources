ある・いる

In this page, we're going to meet our new friends ある and いる.
Both ある and いる is used to mark existance, but.

ある is used for object
いる is used for living being (human, animal, etc) (yes you can use this if your girlfriend is real)

and is attached to particle が

Grammar :

Casual :
Noun が ある (there is noun)(object)
Noun が いる (there is noun)(living being)

Negative Casual :
Noun が ない (there is no noun)(object)
Noun が いない (there is no noun)(living being)


Polite :
Noun が あります (there is noun)(object)
Noun が います (there is noun)(living being)

Negative Polite :
Noun が ありません (there is no noun)(object)
Noun が いません (there is no noun)(living being)

学校がっこう が ある。
There's a school.

>本ほん が ない
There's a book

たけし が います
There's Takeshi.

もりやま が いない
Moriyama is not (here/there)・There's no Moriyama

>彼女かのじょが いる
she's (here/there)

(note : the word >彼女かのじょ can means 2 things, as a pronoun it means she/her, as a noun it can means girlfriend.)

1. furthermore we can also use this grammar to indicate possession depending on the context
2. You can add か after ある (casual)・あります (polite) to turn it into a question.

もりやま : たけし、えんぴつ が ありますか。
Moriyama : Takeshi, do you have a pencil?

たけし : あります.
Takeshi : I have it.

もりやま : たけし、この ゲーム が あるか？。
Moriyama : Takeshi, do you have this game?

たけし : ない
Takeshi : I don't have it

もりやま : たけし, >彼女かのじょ が いますか？
Moriyama : Takeshi, do you have a girlfriend.

たけし : いません.
Takeshi : I don't (a girlfriend)
