Introduction to the Japanese verb

Required verb
食べる = たべる = to eat
見る = みる = to see/watch
聞く = きく = to listen
ラーメン = ramen
映画 = えいが = movie
曲 = きょく = music

Grammar

Noun を verb

1. を originally pronounced as “wo” but when it is used as particle it is pronounced as “o”
2. In Japanese verb that hasn't been conjugated (dictionary form) doesn't have a time indication. but most of the time they’re used to indicate future tense.

Example

ラーメンを食たべる
I eat ramen.

Can also mean

ラーメンを食たべる
I will eat ramen.

曲きょくを聞きく
I (will) listen to a music.

ふみこ は 映画えいが を 見みる
Fumiko (will) watch a movie.

で Particle
used to mark the location where an action takes place/where something is happening.

A で Verb
.
then A will become the location of where the verb is happening.
Example :

喫茶店きっさてん で コーヒ を 飲のむ。 (will) Drink coffee at cafe.

史子ふみこ は 図書館としょかん で 本ほん を 読よむ。
Fumiko (will) reads book at the library.
